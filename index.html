Section 2

Abstract

BOTspiel is a holonomic autonomous curling platform developed to navigate a constrained arena, align to taped field markings, and deploy game stones with repeatable directional control. The system integrates a mecanum-wheel drivetrain with distributed ranging and reflectance sensing, allowing the robot to execute both translational and rotational corrections without requiring a complex localization stack. Three HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensors provide coarse geometric awareness of the starting enclosure and surrounding walls, while three TCRT5000 infrared sensors detect line position and critical scoring thresholds during forward and reverse travel.

The platform architecture is centered around an Arduino Mega, which coordinates sensor polling, drive-state transitions, actuation timing, and safety interlocks within a deterministic control loop. A turntable-based aiming mechanism and a linear pusher actuator convert navigational accuracy into controlled stone deployment. The overall behavior is structured as a staged state machine: orient within the start box, acquire the guide line, follow the path to the hog line, perform a programmed scoring maneuver, and return to the initial region for reset.

This report documents the hardware and software decisions that enabled reliable arena traversal, discusses the tradeoffs imposed by inexpensive sensors and time-based actuation, and presents a framework for evaluating repeatability, robustness, and future system refinement. Detailed results, media documentation, and the full Arduino control script are included to support replication and post-project analysis.

Section 4

Software Architecture & Navigation

The "Smart Compass" Orientation Algorithm

Because calculating rotational yaw from a single ultrasonic sensor per side is highly susceptible to noise, the robot utilizes a "Smart Compass" algorithm based on the physical asymmetry of the arena's 16-inch starting box. Instead of mathematically calculating drift, the robot rotates in place and reads the geometry of the walls. If the Left and Back sensors read under 45cm (detecting the corner walls), but the Right sensor reads out of bounds (detecting the open 4-foot arena), the state machine locks the forward orientation. To counteract ultrasonic blindspots where sound waves bounce off at 45-degree angles, the rotational loop incorporates "momentum," allowing the chassis to coast through dead-zones (999 readings) until the flat walls are re-acquired. Once oriented, a diagonal Back-Left mecanum translation tucks the robot perfectly into the corner, requiring a 2-second continuous verification lock from both sensors to confirm a 0-degree starting heading.

Differential IR Line Following

The robot navigates the arena using a 3-point TCRT5000 IR sensor array (Front-Left, Front-Right, Back-Center). To acquire the line, the robot strafes right until the Back-Center sensor crosses the tape's threshold. Once locked, it utilizes differential steering to drive down the track. If the Front-Left sensor clips the black tape, the right wheels accelerate while the left wheels decelerate, gently swinging the chassis back to center (and vice versa). This inverted logic is also applied when the robot reverses back to the starting box. The critical stop condition for the firing sequence occurs when both front sensors, which straddle the main line, simultaneously detect black tape, indicating the robot has perfectly intersected the perpendicular hog line.

The Scoring State Machine

The master sequence operates strictly sequentially to prevent competing kinematics. Upon reaching the hog line, the drivetrain executes a stopAll() command to settle chassis momentum. The scoring function then activates a turntable to aim, followed by a linear actuator that drives forward at maximum PWM for 3 seconds to deploy the curling stone, and reverses for 3 seconds to retract. To execute diverse tactical shots, the master loop allows for programmed lateral offsets—strafing left or right for a set duration before firing—before returning to the center line to reverse back to the starting box.
